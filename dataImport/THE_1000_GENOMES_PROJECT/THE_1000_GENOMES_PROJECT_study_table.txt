The 1000 Genomes Project aims to provide a deep characterization of human genome sequence variation as a foundation for investigating the relationship between genotype and phenotype. Here results of the pilot phase of the project are shown, designed to develop and compare different strategies for genome-wide sequencing with high-throughput platforms. Three projects were undertaken: low-coverage whole-genome sequencing of 179 individuals from four populations; high-coverage sequencing of two motherÃfatherÃchild trios; and exon-targeted sequencing of 697 individuals from seven populations.
